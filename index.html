An orthopedic specialist will rule your life for the next few weeks, and you will be under the effect of medications for some weeks.

It is your gateway to a pain-free life, improved mobility, and an active routine that was previously compromised due to alleviating joint/bone pain. Therefore, here are the top questions you must ask your orthopedic surgeon during the initial consultation and before getting a personalized surgical plan.

1. Why Do I Need This Procedure?

Orthopedic surgery is recommended in cases where joint pain is not relieved even after being treated with medications, physiotherapy, injection therapy, or exercises. A renowned surgeon will only recommend orthopedic surgery when other methods have failed, and the person is in a lot of pain and discomfort which is limiting their ability to function normally.

The best thing about orthopedic specialists at Swift Surgeries is that they are transparent during the initial consultation, guide you in detail about the whole procedure, and weigh out all the options right in front of you.

2. Is there any Risk in Delaying the Procedure?

The risk of delaying surgical procedures varies according to the type of injury/pain and the seriousness of the joint damage. In case of a torn ligament or arthritis delaying surgical procedure can lead to the worsening of the condition. Similarly, sports-related injuries can alleviate the pain of the patient and increase the seriousness of the case too.

Therefore, Swift Surgeries offers a vast surgical facility with quick appointments without wait times. Our orthopedic specialists understand how a delayed surgery can ruin the condition and might require an even greater timeline to recover.

3. What Will My Recovery Look Like?

The recovery process can vary greatly depending on the type of surgery you're having. It's important not to make assumptions or rely solely on internet searches to understand your recovery. Every individual heals differently, and factors such as age, gender, weight, and overall health can significantly influence how your body recovers. Be sure to ask your surgeon for a detailed explanation of what to expect, from day one through to full recovery.

At Swift Surgeries, we offer a personalized recovery plan so each individual is comfortable with their own recovery journey and isn’t overwhelmed by the comparison.

4. What are your Credentials?

The experience, expertise, and professionalism of the orthopedic surgeon play a vital role in handling the complexities of the surgery. A highly credited surgeon is able to handle unforeseen situations during the surgical procedure and will implement the right solution suitable to each patient’s condition.

At Swift Surgeries, we openly share our surgeons’ credentials and also offer a one-on-one consultation so patients can get confident in their treatment plans.

5. What is the Success Rate of This Procedure?

The questions many patients dread. But know that renowned orthopedic specialists aren’t afraid to answer this question. Rather their consultation includes the pros and cons of the procedure along with other expected scenarios.

6. How many times have you performed this procedure?

Experience matters when it comes to surgery. Asking your surgeon how many times they have performed the specific procedure can help you gauge their expertise and familiarity with the technique. Surgeons who perform a high volume of a particular surgery are often more skilled and efficient, which can positively impact outcomes.

If your surgeon has limited experience with the procedure, you might consider seeking a second opinion from another surgeon who specializes in the surgery and has performed it more frequently. This can provide additional confidence in your treatment plan and ensure you are receiving the best possible care.

7. Should I get a second opinion?

If you have any doubts or uncertainties about your diagnosis or treatment options, seeking a second opinion can be a valuable step. A second opinion can offer clarity, help confirm the initial recommendation, and potentially uncover alternative treatment options you may not have considered.

It's important to feel fully confident in your decision, especially when it comes to surgery. Don’t hesitate to ask your primary physician for a referral to a colleague with specialized experience or consider consulting another expert who focuses on your specific orthopedic condition. Getting another perspective can help ensure you are on the right track and lead to the best outcome for your health.

8. Do you Offer Post-Op Help?

In orthopedic surgery, recovery is a part of the surgical procedure. An Orthopedic Surgeon USA who doesn’t pay attention to the recovery process isn’t someone you should get on board with. Therefore, asking this question will help you evaluate the situation fully. Ask about the kind of post-op help, including consultation, follow-up checkups, medication, recovery plan, and whether or not the facility offers in-house recovery.

You must also ask about the recovery situation even if you are traveling from another location as advanced surgical facilities offer in-house care for patients who must not travel back and forth during the recovery phase.

9. When can I resume normal activities?

This is a common question for many patients, as returning to daily routines, including driving, sports, and other activities, is often a top priority. However, it's crucial to prioritize your recovery to ensure the best possible outcome. Rushing back into activities too soon can not only delay healing but also increase the risk of reinjury, potentially setting back your recovery.

At Sift Surgeries, your surgeon will provide specific guidelines on when it’s safe to resume different activities, considering your unique recovery progress. They also offer a personalized recovery plan based on your lifestyle demands.

10. How Painful will the whole process be?

The pain involved in the surgical procedure isn’t your chance to run but an idea of what to expect. All surgeries hurt, even the ones performed under expert care. A team who is open about the pain levels and does not hide the realities is the right team to work with. This question will also help you discuss the medication plan and request sick leaves from work.

Orthopedic surgery is a serious medical procedure that must be well-planned. Feel free to reach out to your orthopedic surgeon in case of any query so you go well-prepared into the surgical suite!
